02

How I think.

Ship to learn.

A live experiment with 100 users teaches more than a 40-page spec. I default to shipping small, measuring fast, and iterating with evidence.

Data-informed, not data-paralyzed.

Data validates direction, it shouldn't replace conviction. I use metrics to sharpen decisions, not to avoid making them.

AI is a capability, not a feature.

The best AI products don't announce themselves. I integrate intelligence into existing workflows where it compounds user value, not where it looks impressive.

Own the outcome, not the output.

A shipped PRD is not success. A moved metric is. I measure my work by what changed for the user, not what was delivered to engineering.

FIFA World Cup'26 Simulator

Problem

Sports fans follow the World Cup passionately but existing apps — SofaScore, Fotmob, OneFootball — only show data. No prediction, no simulation, no social bragging rights. The fan has no way to play alongside the tournament.

Approach

Full-stack Next.js PWA with six core features: an interactive 48-team bracket simulator with shareable URLs, FIFA-style player cards with radar comparison charts, a live “If This Happens” group stage qualifier with real-time odds, a tournament story timeline (matchday-by-matchday narrative), a Glory Index power ranking, and a WC Wrapped personalised daily summary.
