The brief

Optimus is a neobank offering checking accounts, savings accounts, and a debit card to retail banking customers. The brief: design a Family Hub that enables the primary account holder to create add-on cards for their spouse, children, parents, or other family members.

The brief is simple on the surface. The design challenge is not. In India in 2025, the primary competitor for Family Hub is not another neobank. It is the UPI transfer — a behaviour that is free, instant, and already deeply embedded in how households move money. Family Hub has to earn adoption against a default behaviour that already works.

Strategic Frame

Family Hub is a household retention and acquisition engine, not a feature. A single-holder account churns when one person switches banks. A household account with two or more active linked cards churns only when an entire household switches simultaneously — requiring every member to move to a new card, new app, and new habits at the same time. That compounding switching cost is the business case.

01
Hypothesis & KPIs

Core hypothesis: Family Hub will increase household-level retention and grow card transaction volume per account by converting single-holder accounts into multi-card household relationships. The switching cost multiplies with every active member added.

The emotional hypothesis is equally important: a family member who receives UPI transfers is a recipient. A family member with a named Optimus card is a participant. Family Hub earns adoption not just because it is useful — but because it changes how members relate to money in their household.

The hypothesis holds only if two conditions are both true: primary holders trust a digital neobank enough to link a family member, and linked members use the card for regular spend rather than defaulting to UPI. Validation is designed to test these two conditions before full build.

03
Validation Experiments

Three experiments. Each tests an assumption that would kill Family Hub if wrong. No experiment is run if the one before it fails — the sequence is a gate, not a parallel track.

Experiment 01 — Run first
Primary Holder Trust Interviews
“Primary account holders will link a family member to their Optimus account if given spend controls and real-time visibility.”
Method

15 primary Optimus account holders, married or with dependents. 45-min semi-structured interview on current family finance behaviour, trust concerns, and concept walkthrough of spend limits and freeze controls.

Success: 10 of 15 say they would link a family member given the controls shown. Trust concern is addressable by product design.
If it fails: Fewer than 10 of 15 → trust barrier is structural. Do not proceed to MVP. Reframe as a waitlist product and run a 3-month trust-building campaign before re-testing. If refusal is neobank-specific distrust rather than product design → Family Hub is premature for Optimus’s current stage. Escalate before committing engineering.
Experiment 02 — No infrastructure required
Card vs UPI Spend Proxy Analysis
“Existing transaction data contains a reliable proxy signal for whether Family Hub members will use a card rather than defaulting to UPI.”
Method

Pull 90 days of transaction data for all active primary account holders. Segment into card POS, UPI outbound, and other. Calculate card POS % per user. Identify the Hook 1 cohort (repeat bank transfer senders) and measure their card POS % vs platform average.

Success: 35%+ of active users spend 40%+ on card POS. Card usage increases with account tenure.
If it fails: Fewer than 25% card-first → Optimus’s user base is predominantly UPI-native. GMV thesis is structurally weak. Either redesign Family Hub to include UPI visibility, or accept that interchange revenue will be materially lower than 1.8x target. Card POS not increasing with tenure → habit formation is not occurring. Investigate card experience friction before adding a second card layer.
Experiment 03 — Run after Exp 01 passes
Add Member Flow Prototype Usability Test
“A primary holder can complete the invite and spend limit setup flow in under 5 minutes without assistance.”
Method

Medium-fidelity prototype of the complete add member flow. Moderated usability test with 12 participants. Task: create a card for your spouse with a Rs. 15,000 monthly limit. Measure time on task, completion rate, drop-off screen.

Success: 9 of 12 complete the flow. Average time under 5 minutes. No single screen generates confusion in 25%+ of participants.
If it fails: Below 70% completion → identify highest drop-off screen and redesign before engineering begins. Spend limit configuration is primary drop-off → replace free-text input with slider (Rs. 5K / 15K / 25K / 50K presets).
04
Capabilities (MVP)

MVP only. The three capabilities without which Family Hub fails: sub-account card issuance with real-time limit enforcement, member KYC and onboarding, and dual real-time transaction notifications. Phase 2 and Phase 3 are gated on MVP performance metrics being met.

Primary Holder — Member Management
  • Invite a family member by mobile number
  • Set a monthly spend limit per member
  • Freeze or unfreeze a member card instantly
  • View member transaction history in real time
  • Remove a member and deactivate their card
Primary Holder — Visibility
  • Real-time push on every member card transaction
  • Family Hub dashboard with all active members
  • Monthly spend summary per member
  • Remaining limit visible at a glance
Member — Own Card Experience
  • Download Optimus app with own login
  • Set own PIN (primary holder cannot see it)
  • View own transaction history and balance
  • Manage card settings independently
  • Own spending notifications
Platform — MVP Only
  • Member KYC via Aadhaar + PAN (digital)
  • Sub-account card issuance with limit enforcement
  • Real-time spend limit tracking
  • Dual notification architecture (primary + member)
  • Privacy boundary: member cannot see primary balance
Design Principle: The member app is not a lite version of the primary holder’s app. It is a product designed around a specific emotional truth: this is your card, your history, your financial identity. A member who feels ownership over their card and their data is a member who uses it. The privacy boundary — members cannot see the primary account balance — is not a restriction. It is the design decision that makes the member experience feel like independence rather than supervision.

06
Discovery Hooks

Three hooks at launch. One behavioural trigger, one surface placement, one notification trigger. Additional hooks are activated only after in-app adoption rate is measured from the first three — more hooks firing simultaneously produces measurement noise and user fatigue, not more adoption.

01
Behavioural Trigger — Highest Intent
Repeat Bank Transfer to Same Recipient

Trigger: Primary holder sends 3+ bank transfers to the same beneficiary in a calendar month, combined value exceeding Rs. 3,000. Fires on the transfer confirmation screen immediately after the third qualifying transfer.

“You have sent Rs. X to [Name] this month. Give them a card of their own instead — free, with a limit you control.”

Why this is highest-intent: the user is already doing the exact behaviour Family Hub replaces. Note: fires on bank transfers only, not UPI — UPI is a problem Family Hub doesn’t solve.

02
Surface — Always Present
Family Tab in Bottom Navigation

Always present, no condition required. Pre-setup: feature introduction. Post-setup: Family Hub dashboard directly.

“Stop sending transfers. Give them a card of their own.”

Lead with the behaviour being replaced (transfers) and the emotional upgrade (their own card). Free is stated explicitly — removes the fee friction that kills add-on card adoption at traditional banks.

03
Notification — Post-Salary Credit
Salary Credit Nudge

Trigger: Primary holder receives a salary credit above Rs. 25,000. Sent once only, on the first qualifying salary credit after launch. Suppressed if Family Hub is already set up.

Push title: “Your salary is in.”
Body: “Manage household expenses smarter this month. Set up Family Hub to give your family their own cards with spending controls.”

Why post-salary: highest financial management intent moment in the month. Users are actively thinking about budgets at exactly this moment.

Guardrails: User who has set up Family Hub never sees any discovery hook again. User who dismisses the same hook twice is suppressed for 90 days. No user receives more than 2 discovery push notifications in any 30-day window.
07
Metrics & North Star
North Star — Two Definitions

MVP North Star: Number of Active Family Hub Accounts — a primary account with at least one active add-on card, where active = at least one card POS transaction in the last 30 days on both cards. Requiring two cards for the MVP north star would make it unreachable until Phase 2 ships.

Steady-State North Star (Phase 2+): Number of Active Household Accounts — at least two active add-on cards. At two active members, the switching cost becomes genuinely compounding. Neither north star can be inflated by card issuance without use. A card issued to a member who defaults to UPI counts zero.
